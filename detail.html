About Food Waste

How Much?

In 2019, it has been shown that Indonesia is the second largest producer of food waste in the world after Saudi Arabia. In 2021, the National Waste Management Information System recorded that Indonesia's food waste reached 46.35 million tons on a national scale.

Impact

GHG emissions produced by Indonesia over the last 20 years reached 1,702.9 Megatons CO2 equivalent or equivalent to 7.29% of average GHG emissions per year. The average greenhouse gas emissions generated from 1 ton of food waste are 4.3 times that of leftover. More precisely, of the five stages of the food supply chain, the largest contributor to gas emissions comes from the consumption stage.
